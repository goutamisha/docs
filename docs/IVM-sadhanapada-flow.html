01 The pipeline

Each box is a stage, each arrow is a decision that moves the application to another stage. Self actions (Hold, Cancel, Request Info, Record Opinion, Callback, No Answer, Start Trial, Verify Documents, Enable Arrivals) hold the same stage and are left off the arrows so the flow stays legible. Arrows marked guarded only become available once the decision's guard holds.

02 Reading the flow

  • Happy path. Eligibility Screening, Outreach, Video Call, First Review, Final Review, Arrivals, Approved.
  • Interview branch. Video Call can route to an Additional Interview, which feeds back into First Review.
  • Trial branch. First Review and Final Review can send an applicant to Trial, and Trial rejoins at Final Review.
  • Health lane. Health Assessment and Exception Review open at First Review and run concurrently, they do not block the main line until a review gate needs them.
  • Rejected / Reassessment. A holding stage. It can reassess back into Screening, First Review, or Final Review, or confirm the Rejected outcome.
Terminal outcomes

Approved and Approved with Trial Period (both from Arrivals), and Rejected (from Rejected / Reassessment). These are the only three ways an application leaves the pipeline.
